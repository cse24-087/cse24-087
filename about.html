Our History & Legacy

About Manchester City FC

Founded in 1880 as St. Mark's (West Gorton), Manchester City has grown from a local club into one of the most iconic teams in world football. Renamed Manchester City in 1894, the club has a rich history deeply rooted in the heart of Manchester.

The Etihad Stadium, their modern home since 2003, stands as a symbol of ambition and progress. With a seating capacity of over 53,000, it's more than just a stadium — it's a fortress where football dreams are built.

Under the visionary leadership of manager Pep Guardiola, City has transformed into a global powerhouse. The team is renowned for its dynamic, possession-based style of play, and has secured numerous Premier League titles, domestic cups, and a historic UEFA Champions League title.

Off the pitch, Manchester City is committed to community outreach and youth development through the City Football Group. Their influence extends globally, promoting values of teamwork, innovation, and unity.

Whether you're a lifelong fan or a newcomer to the sky-blue legacy, Manchester City welcomes everyone to be part of their journey.

Club History Highlights

1880
Founded as St. Mark's (West Gorton), later becoming Ardwick Association Football Club in 1887
1894
Officially renamed Manchester City Football Club
1937
Won their first First Division title
1968
Secured their first First Division title in the modern era
2003
Moved to the City of Manchester Stadium (now Etihad Stadium)
2008
Acquired by Abu Dhabi United Group, beginning a new era of success
2012
Won their first Premier League title in dramatic fashion with Aguero's last-minute goal
2023
Achieved the historic Treble - Premier League, FA Cup, and Champions League
